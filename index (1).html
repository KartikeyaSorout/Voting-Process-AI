Process Overview

Indian Election Timeline

The complete journey from announcement to sworn-in government — visualized.

1
Pre-Election

📢 Election Announcement

Day 0 — Official Start

The Election Commission of India (ECI) issues the Model Code of Conduct. Political parties and candidates must follow strict guidelines from this date.

Key Facts
  • Model Code of Conduct comes into effect immediately
  • No new government schemes may be announced
  • All political advertising requires ECI approval
  • ECI is an autonomous constitutional body (Article 324)
2
Pre-Election

📋 Voter List & Registration

Ongoing — Electoral Roll Update

Citizens aged 18+ can register to vote or update their details on the NVSP portal or via Form 6. The final electoral roll is published before voting.

How to Register
  • Visit voters.eci.gov.in or NVSP portal
  • Fill Form 6 (new registration) or Form 8 (correction)
  • Submit Aadhaar, address proof, photo
  • Track status online within 30 days
3
Pre-Election

📝 Nomination Filing

~Day 20 — Candidates Register

Candidates file nomination papers with the Returning Officer. A security deposit is required. Nominations are scrutinized and candidates may withdraw within the deadline.

Key Facts
  • Security deposit: ₹25,000 for general; ₹12,500 for SC/ST
  • Deposit forfeited if candidate gets less than 1/6 of valid votes
  • Affidavit declaring criminal records, assets is mandatory
  • Final list of candidates published after withdrawal deadline
4
Pre-Election

📣 Campaign Period

~Day 25–50 — Active Campaigning

Parties and candidates campaign across constituencies. Rallies, door-to-door outreach, media campaigns — all regulated by ECI's spending limits.

Spending Limits
  • Lok Sabha: ₹95 lakh per candidate (large states)
  • All expenses tracked and submitted to ECI
  • Silent period: 48 hours before polling day
  • Exit polls banned until last phase concludes
5
Election Day

🗳️ Voting Day(s)

Phase 1–7 — The Big Day

Voters visit their designated polling booth (as per Voter ID / Aadhaar) and cast their vote using the Electronic Voting Machine (EVM). The process is phased across India's vast geography.

Voting Process
  • Bring Voter ID card or any of 12 approved photo IDs
  • Mark finger with indelible ink to prevent double-voting
  • Press the blue button on EVM for your candidate
  • VVPAT slip visible for 7 seconds as confirmation
  • Polling hours typically 7 AM – 6 PM
6
Post-Election

🔢 Vote Counting

Count Day — Results Declared

EVMs are transported to secure counting centers. Counting begins at 8 AM on count day. Results are declared constituency by constituency by Returning Officers.

Counting Process
  • EVMs opened in presence of candidates' agents
  • Each round counts votes for one polling booth
  • Postal ballots counted first
  • ECI declares winners on official website in real-time
7
Post-Election

🏛️ Government Formation

~2 Weeks After Results

The party/coalition with majority (272+ seats) forms the government. The President invites the leader to form the government. The Prime Minister and Council of Ministers are sworn in.

Formation Steps
  • President invites majority party/coalition leader
  • PM takes oath of office & secrecy
  • Council of Ministers appointed (max 15% of Lok Sabha strength)
  • Floor test may be required if majority is unclear
Step-by-Step

Complete Voter Guide

Everything a first-time (or seasoned) voter needs to know.

01

Check Eligibility

You must be an Indian citizen, aged 18 or above on the qualifying date (January 1st of the election year), and ordinarily resident in the constituency where you wish to register.

🟢 Required: Aadhaar / Proof of Age / Address Proof
02

Visit the NVSP Portal

Go to voters.eci.gov.in or the Voter Helpline App. Click "Register as New Voter" and select Form 6. You can also register at your nearest BLO (Booth Level Officer).

🌐 Online: voters.eci.gov.in | 📱 App: Voter Helpline
03

Fill & Submit Form 6

Provide your name, date of birth, address, and upload a passport-size photo. Attach supporting documents. Submit online — you'll receive an acknowledgement reference number.

📎 Upload: Photo, Age Proof, Address Proof
04

Track Your Application

Use your reference number on the NVSP portal to track the status. BLO may visit your address for verification. Processing takes up to 30 days.

⏱️ Processing: Up to 30 days
05

Receive Voter ID (EPIC)

Once approved, you'll receive your Elector's Photo Identity Card (EPIC) — also known as Voter ID. You can also download an e-EPIC from the portal.

✅ Download e-EPIC instantly after approval

📍 Find Your Polling Booth

Your assigned polling booth is printed on your Voter ID. You can also find it on the ECI website by entering your EPIC number.

Search on ECI →

🪪 Accepted ID Documents

  • Voter ID Card (EPIC)
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Passport
  • Driving Licence
  • PAN Card
  • MNREGA Job Card
  • Bank / Post Office Passbook (with photo)
  • Employee ID (Govt/PSU)

📰 Research Your Candidates

All candidate affidavits (criminal history, education, assets) are publicly available on the ECI website. Use KnowYourCandidate app to review before voting.

⏰ Polling Hours

General polling hours are 7 AM to 6 PM. Urban and remote areas may have different hours. Check your booth's schedule on the ECI site.

🚶

Arrive at Your Polling Booth

Carry your Voter ID or approved photo ID. Locate your booth early — especially in congested areas. Queue-management apps from ECI show real-time wait times.

✅

Identity Verification

Polling officers verify your name on the voter list and check your photo ID. Your name is marked in the register and you sign/thumb-print.

🖊️

Indelible Ink

Indelible ink is applied on the left index finger — it cannot be washed off for days. This prevents duplicate voting across phases.

🗳️

Cast Your Vote on EVM

In the voting compartment, press the blue button next to your chosen candidate's name and party symbol on the EVM ballot unit.

📜

VVPAT Confirmation

The VVPAT (Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail) machine shows a paper slip for 7 seconds confirming your vote. This slip drops into a sealed box — verifiable in disputes.

🏠

Done! Your Vote is Counted

You have fulfilled your democratic duty. The entire process takes under 5 minutes at most booths. EVMs are sealed and stored until counting day.

🔢 How Are Votes Counted?

Counting happens on a single day for all phases. EVMs are brought to counting centers under strict security. Counting agents from each candidate's party are present to observe every round.

🏆 First Past the Post (FPTP)

India uses the FPTP system — the candidate with the most votes in a constituency wins, even without a majority. This is also called a "simple plurality" system.

🏛️ Forming the Government

For a party to form government alone, it needs 272 seats (simple majority of 543). Coalitions are common — the leader of the largest alliance is invited by the President to form the government.

📊 Reading the Results

Results come in constituency-by-constituency. Running tallies on ECI's website show party-wise totals in real time. Most major results are declared within 6–10 hours of counting starting at 8 AM.

🔒 Is EVM Hackable?

EVMs are standalone devices — they are not connected to internet, WiFi, or Bluetooth. They use one-time programmable chips manufactured by government units (ECIL & BEL). The Supreme Court has upheld their reliability.

📦 Storage & Transport

After voting, EVMs are sealed with multiple seals, signed by candidate agents, and stored in strong rooms under round-the-clock CRPF guard and CCTV surveillance until counting day.

🧪 First-Level Checking (FLC)

Before elections, all EVMs undergo FLC in the presence of political party representatives. Mock polls are conducted to test accuracy. Any faulty machines are rejected.
